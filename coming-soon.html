Steam Coming Soon Page Guide

How to set up your Coming Soon page, when to launch it, and how to maximize wishlists before release.

Why Coming Soon Matters

Your Coming Soon page is your primary wishlist-building tool. The earlier you launch it, the more time you have to accumulate wishlists — and wishlists directly impact your launch visibility.

The 2-Week Minimum

Steam requires your page to be in "Coming Soon" state for at least 2 weeks before you can release. Plan accordingly — you can't rush this.

When to Launch Your Coming Soon Page

Rule of thumb: Launch your Coming Soon page as early as possible — ideally 6-12 months before release. More time = more wishlists.

Ideal Timeline

1

6-12 months before release

Launch Coming Soon page. You need playable footage and solid store assets, but not a finished game.

2

Ongoing

Drive traffic to your page. Social media, devlogs, demos, events, press coverage — all funnel to wishlists.

3

1-2 months before release

Submit your build for review. Finalize your release date. Ramp up marketing.

4

2 weeks minimum

Maintain Coming Soon state while build is reviewed. Can't release until this period passes.

5

Release day

Once approved and 2-week minimum is met, you can release at any time you choose.

Coming Soon Page Requirements

Before your page can go live as "Coming Soon," you need to complete the store presence checklist:

  • All capsule images — Header, small, main capsule at minimum
  • At least 5 screenshots — Actual gameplay, not concept art
  • Trailer — Gameplay footage strongly recommended
  • About This Game description — Complete, no placeholders
  • System requirements — All required fields filled
  • Genre tags — At least 3-5 relevant tags
  • Early Access disclosures — If applicable

No placeholder content: Your Coming Soon page should look like a real store page. "Screenshots coming soon" or "TBD" will delay approval.

Wishlist Optimization Tips

Page Optimization

  • Lead with gameplay — First screenshot and trailer should show what players DO
  • Clear genre signals — Use tags and visuals that match player expectations
  • Compelling short description — This appears in search results and lists
  • Add your Discord — Build community before launch

Traffic Strategies

  • Steam Next Fest — Demo events drive massive wishlists
  • Regular devlogs — Steam followers get notified
  • Social media — Always link to Steam page, not other stores
  • Press/influencer outreach — Early coverage builds anticipation
  • Other games — Cross-promote if you have previous releases

What Converts to Wishlists

  • Clear gameplay hook in first 5 seconds of trailer
  • Screenshots that show the actual game loop
  • Specific feature list (not vague promises)
  • Estimated release window (even "2026" is better than nothing)
  • Playable demo availability

Common Mistakes

  • Waiting too long — Launching Coming Soon 1 month before release leaves no time for wishlists
  • Cinematic-only trailer — Players want to see gameplay
  • Vague description — "An epic adventure" tells players nothing
  • No release estimate — "When it's ready" frustrates potential fans
  • Ignoring the page — Update regularly to keep followers engaged

After Going Live

Once your Coming Soon page is approved and public:

  1. Track your wishlists — Steamworks shows daily wishlist additions
  2. Update regularly — New screenshots, trailers, devlogs keep momentum
  3. Respond to followers — Community engagement matters
  4. Participate in events — Steam Next Fest, seasonal sales, etc.
  5. Don't change your page constantly — Each edit needs re-publishing
