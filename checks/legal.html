Steam Legal & Copyright

How to avoid trademark issues, handle game comparisons, and properly attribute assets.

Legal issues can delay or kill your launch. Trademark infringement, unlicensed assets, or improper game comparisons will get your page rejected — or worse, your game removed.

Referencing Other Games

You want to say your game is "like Dark Souls" — but be careful how you phrase it.

❌ Problematic

"Better than Dark Souls"

"The next Dark Souls"

"Dark Souls killer"

✓ Acceptable

"Challenging combat inspired by souls-like games"

"For fans of difficult action RPGs"

"Souls-like mechanics with a twist"

The Rule

Reference the genre or style, not the trademark. "Souls-like" has become a genre term. "Dark Souls" is a trademark owned by FromSoftware/Bandai Namco.

Fan Games & IP

You need explicit permission. Fan games using existing IP (Pokémon, Mario, etc.) require written authorization from the rights holder. "It's a tribute" or "I'm not making money" doesn't protect you.

  • Fan games without permission are typically rejected or removed
  • Even "inspired by" can be risky if characters or names are too similar
  • Get permission in writing before development, not after

Asset Attribution

Third-Party Assets

If you use assets from the Unity Store, Unreal Marketplace, or freelancers, check your license terms. Some require attribution. Include credits where required.

  • Music — Ensure you have commercial rights, not just personal use
  • Sound effects — Many "free" sounds require attribution
  • Art assets — Read the license, especially for characters
  • Fonts — Many fonts aren't licensed for games

Things to Avoid

  • Using trademarked names in your game title
  • Screenshots showing other companies' logos
  • Claiming endorsement or affiliation you don't have
  • Using real celebrities or public figures without permission
  • Copying store page text from other games

What Our Check Looks For

  • Trademarked game names used improperly
  • Comparison claims ("better than X", "X killer")
  • Fan game indicators without licensing mentions
  • Missing attribution keywords when asset packs are mentioned
  • Potentially problematic IP references
